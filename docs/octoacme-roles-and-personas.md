# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Engineering Lead

### Role Summary
Engineering Leads provide technical direction, mentor developers, and partner with Project Managers to ensure delivery aligns with architectural standards and timelines.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain technical architecture and standards
- Review and approve technical designs and pull requests
- Partner with Project Managers to surface technical risks and dependencies
- Mentor and support developers in best practices
- Coordinate with DevOps Engineers for deployment readiness

### Goals
- Maintain a healthy, scalable codebase
- Enable the team to deliver high-quality software at a sustainable pace
- Reduce technical debt while balancing delivery commitments

### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews and architecture decision records
- Sprint planning and backlog refinement with engineering team
- Escalations to Project Manager for scope or dependency issues

---

## Product Owner

### Role Summary
Product Owners represent the voice of the customer and business within the delivery team. They work alongside Product Managers to translate strategy into a prioritized, actionable backlog and ensure the team builds the right things.

### Responsibilities
- Own and maintain the product backlog, ensuring items are well-defined and prioritized
- Write and refine user stories and acceptance criteria in collaboration with developers and QA
- Serve as the primary decision-maker for scope and feature trade-offs during delivery
- Validate delivered features against acceptance criteria before sprint close
- Collaborate with the Project Manager on schedule and milestone alignment

### Goals
- Ensure the team always works on the highest-value items
- Minimize rework through clear, upfront requirements
- Act as the bridge between stakeholders and the delivery team

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint reviews to inspect and accept/reject work
- Backlog refinement sessions with developers and Business Analysts
- Regular alignment meetings with Product Managers and stakeholders

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Assurance (QA) Leads oversee testing strategy, coordinate testing efforts across the team, and ensure that deliverables meet defined quality and acceptance standards before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the test strategy, test plans, and quality standards
- Coordinate manual and automated testing activities across sprints
- Collaborate with developers to identify testable acceptance criteria early
- Track and triage defects, escalating blockers to the Project Manager
- Work with DevOps Engineers to integrate automated tests into the CI/CD pipeline
- Sign off on release readiness from a quality perspective

### Goals
- Ensure every release meets quality and reliability standards
- Shift quality left by embedding testing practices early in the development lifecycle
- Reduce defect escape rate to production

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and retrospectives to surface quality trends
- Bug triage and defect review meetings
- Release readiness reviews with Project Manager and Engineering Lead

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between stakeholders and the delivery team. They gather, document, and clarify requirements, ensuring everyone has a shared understanding of what needs to be built and why.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit requirements from stakeholders through interviews, workshops, and process analysis
- Document requirements as user stories, use cases, or process flows for the Product Owner
- Analyze current business processes and identify areas for improvement
- Facilitate communication between business stakeholders and the engineering team
- Support the Product Owner in refining and prioritizing the backlog
- Validate that delivered solutions meet the original business needs

### Goals
- Ensure requirements are complete, clear, and actionable before development begins
- Reduce ambiguity and rework caused by misunderstood requirements
- Enable informed prioritization decisions through business process insight

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Backlog refinement with the Product Owner and development team
- Handoff documents, process diagrams, and acceptance criteria sign-offs

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers design, build, and maintain the infrastructure, deployment pipelines, and tooling that enable reliable, continuous delivery of software. They act as a force multiplier for the development team's ability to ship safely and quickly.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines for automated build, test, and deployment
- Manage cloud infrastructure, environments, and configuration as code
- Monitor system health, reliability, and performance across environments
- Partner with the Engineering Lead to ensure deployments meet architectural and security standards
- Collaborate with the QA Lead to integrate automated test suites into delivery pipelines
- Establish and maintain incident response runbooks and on-call procedures

### Goals
- Enable the team to deploy with confidence and at high frequency
- Reduce mean time to recovery (MTTR) for production incidents
- Improve observability and minimize toil through automation

### Typical Communication
- Deployment readiness reviews with Engineering Lead and Project Manager
- Incident post-mortems and runbook updates
- Sprint planning participation to surface infrastructure dependencies

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers coordinate organizational and process change initiatives. They ensure transitions are well-planned, clearly communicated, and land smoothly across affected teams and stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Develop change management plans covering communication, training, and adoption strategies
- Assess the impact of proposed changes on people, processes, and systems
- Facilitate stakeholder engagement and manage resistance to change
- Coordinate with the Project Manager to align change activities with project milestones
- Track adoption metrics and feedback post-rollout, escalating issues as needed
- Maintain a change log and communicate status to leadership and impacted teams

### Goals
- Maximize adoption and minimize disruption during transitions
- Ensure teams are prepared and supported before, during, and after changes go live
- Reduce change-related incidents and rollbacks through proactive planning

### Typical Communication
- Change readiness reviews with Project Manager and stakeholders
- Communication plans distributed to affected teams ahead of releases
- Post-implementation reviews to assess adoption and gather feedback
